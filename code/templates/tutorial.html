Overview

The design of IWE follows a straightforward SELECT->DISPLAY interface model

The Selectors Panel on the left filters issues for the display of Workflow, Investigations and Time Trend

The Workflow view in the middle provides further selection. Click on the green Select button on the top-right, it filters issues together with Selectors panel

Click on the light-blueTime Trend buttons, metrics of issues will be visualized along the time axis

  • The button on the Workflow View visualizes the time trend of Number of issues
  • The button on the Resolution View visualizes the time trend of Fix Rate of issues
  • The button on the Resolve Time View visualizes the Resolve Time of most issues (here we use 90% as a representative) over time

Details

Workflow

The image below visualizes the status transition of issues in Mozilla

In the tree-like graph, the edge and radius of nodes indicate the number of issues that went through this transition

The length of edge indicates the median time spent on this transition

The sub-nodes are ordered by the number of issues. The node on the left is always the biggest, indicating the status that most issues went through

Different transitions are merged when the time spent on it are similar. Otherwise they are separated.
For example, in the figure above, we have transition UNCONF->NEW->RESOLVED (tr1) and transition UNCONF->NEW->RESOLVED->VERIFIED (tr2). The time spent on UNCONF->NEW in tr1 and tr2 are similar (20% is the threshold), so the status NEW in tr1 and tr2 are merged (we considered them a same workflow). But from NEW to RESOLVED, the time spent on this transition in tr1 and tr2 is of huge difference. Therefore status NEW has two sub-nodes RESOLVED

Investigations

The Resolution View and Resolve Time View provides function of comparison.
Click on Draw, it will only add a single bar (or line). Click Clear to clear all the bars.

Time Trend

The x-Axis of the time trend view can either be the report time or the resolve time of the issues.
The anomaly of the black line in the figure above shows that Mozilla resolved (batch-closed) nearly 10k issues in a week in October, 2005
